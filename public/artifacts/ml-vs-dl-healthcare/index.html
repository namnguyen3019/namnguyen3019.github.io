Industry Overview · 01

The Healthcare Data Landscape

The shape of the data decides the tool.

Structured Standardized

  • EHR — histories, meds, lab results
  • Billing — ICD-10 & CPT codes
  • Demographics — population-health metrics

→ Tabular & auditable — home of classical ML

Unstructured High-volume

  • Imaging — DICOM X-rays, MRIs, CT
  • Clinical text — notes & discharge summaries
  • Genomics & real-time IoT vitals

→ Pixels & signals — where deep learning earns its cost

Traditional ML · Epic Systems

Catching Sepsis Early

The Epic Sepsis Model — real-time early warning inside the EHR at hundreds of U.S. hospitals.

The stakes

~1M U.S. sepsis cases / year

A top driver of hospital mortality — but the signs hide in routine vitals.

Company

Epic Systems — the leading U.S. EHR vendor. ESM runs inside its platform, e.g. U-Michigan Medicine.

What it does

Continuously scores patients and alerts when sepsis risk crosses a threshold.

Algorithm · Justification

Logistic Regression — and Why It Fits

The model

  • Penalized logistic regression — interpretable, not a neural net
  • Features — vitals, labs, meds & demographics → one weighted risk score

Why ML wins here

  • Structured data — logistic regression's sweet spot
  • Interpretable — coefficients show why a patient was flagged
  • Efficient — real-time scoring, no heavy compute

Honest caveat

External validation found low positive predictive value and a high false-alert burden — the gap between promise and real-world performance.

Wong et al. (2021), JAMA Internal Medicine

Deep Learning · Google Health

Preventing Blindness at Scale

A CNN grading retinal photos for diabetic retinopathy, where eye specialists are scarce.

The bottleneck

Too few specialists per patient

A leading cause of preventable blindness — screening is scarce in rural India & SE Asia.

Company

Google Research, deployed via Sankara Nethralaya (India) & clinics in Thailand.

What it does

Auto-grades fundus photos for referable DR — screening at scale, catching disease early.

Architecture · Justification

A CNN — and Why ML Falls Short

Architecture & data

  • Inception-v3 CNN — pretrained, then fine-tuned
  • ~128,000 images, each graded by 3–7 of 54 ophthalmologists
  • Subtle spatial signs no hand-crafted feature captures

Why ML plateaus

  • Manual features discard fine spatial detail
  • Can't match learned convolutional filters
  • CNN hit 90%+ sensitivity & specificity — ML plateaus below

Gulshan et al. (2016), JAMA

Trade-offs — and Could They Swap?

DL for sepsis?

Theoretically yes — but loses interpretability the FDA requires. Viable, not yet appropriate.

ML for retinopathy?

Technically yes — but misses microaneurysms & hemorrhages. A safety failure.

Strategic Recommendation

Roadmap to 2031

Sequence the investment — then let the five-year horizon pull it forward.

PHASE 1
Year 0–1

ML First

Interpretable models on structured problems. Build clean pipelines, governance, clinician trust & ROI.

PHASE 2
Year 1–2

Targeted DL Pilots

DL only where accuracy wins & big labeled sets exist — imaging. Human-in-the-loop mandatory.

PHASE 3
Year 3–5

Multimodal AI

Fuse both — risk scores + note NLP + imaging. Add federated learning across institutions.

Beyond — the five-year horizon · 2026–2031

Multimodal Models

One model ingesting labs, notes, imaging & wearables — Med-PaLM 2, Nuance DAX. Standard for tertiary care.

Federated Learning

Share gradients, not raw data — high-accuracy models without HIPAA violations. The biggest unlock ahead.

Regulatory Maturation

FDA SaMD & EU AI Act tighten — continuous monitoring & bias audits. Early governance wins.

Ethical Reflection

The Ethical Reckoning

Three risk zones — and the four commitments that answer them.

01 Model Bias

Obermeyer (2019): a model learned to discriminate based on flawed labels. 84% cut in racial bias after fixing.

02 Liability

When harm occurs, who's liable? ML's coefficients can be audited; DL's black box cannot be defended in court.

03 Privacy

"Anonymized" data can be re-identified. HIPAA is a floor. Differential privacy & strict access mandatory.

Four commitments

  • Human-in-the-loop — clinician confirms before action.
  • Continuous audit — track by race, sex, age & insurance.
  • Compliance — FDA SaMD, HIPAA & EU AI Act as floor.
  • Model cards — public docs on training & limits.
